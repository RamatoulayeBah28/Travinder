# Brainstorming
## Language Learning App

- Fetches from a language API to have the user learn new vocabulary in a target language


## Budget Tracker App
- Easy budget planner for college students specifically that takes into account, student loans, groceries (based on meal plan option), etc
- Section with the ability to save who owes you money (weekend out, splitting the bill etc)
- Gives the user a detailed summary of their monthly spending (dashboard for navigation bar)
- Tips navigation bar with new tips on how to be financially literate
- Use an API that tells you how you could save for the next month (genZ language: girl do you really need new clothes next month? You already bought a new dress last month, save those 50 bucks!)

## Travinder 
- Tinder but for travel destinations based on your budget, the types of activities you want to do, how long you are going for and whether you are going with friends, family, significant other etc
- The user can swipe left or right on different destinations and get a match that closely meet the user needs
- Fetches from an API to know the average plane flight right now from your current location
- Nagivation bar with suggestions of cheap flights to places around you along with suggested activities to do there

## Study Buddy
- A pomodoro app that holds you accountable thanks to your study buddy that gets mad at you / scares you / cries whenever you pick up your phone to discourage you from using your phone and procrastinate.
- I'm picturing a face time type of app.

## Gym Buddy
- An app to keep all your gym progress (measurements, weight etc)
- Keep track of how heavy you lift for each exercise and tells you whether you are progressing or not (reps/sets/speed etc)
- Usually in most fitness app these functionalities are not free

# Evaluating App Ideas
- Budget Tracker
- Travinder

Mobile: How uniquely mobile is the product experience? What makes your app more than a glorified website?
- Budget: People carry around their phone wherever they go so it's easier to input any expense
- Travinder: Checks your current location to suggest travel plans + swiping requires an app


Story: How compelling is the story around this app once completed? How clear is the value of this app to your audience? How well would your friends or peers respond to this product idea?

- I showed my brainstorming list to some friends and travinder seems to receive a lot more posititve reaction due to the connection to Tinder and how college students like to travel for cheap.

Market: How large or unique is the market for this app? What's the size and scale of your potential user base? Does this app provide huge value to a niche group of people? Do you have a well-defined audience of people for this app?

- The budget tracker app has a smaller audience 18-25 since it targets mostly college students/young adults/GenZ

- For travinder the audience is larger but still under the 40 bar since Tinder tends to have a younger population group. I would say 18-40.

Habit: How habit-forming or addictive is this app?
How frequently would an average user open and use this app? Does an average user just consume your app or do they create?

- Swiping is a very addictive behavior however people are not necessarily looking to travel every time (not very frequent)
- The budget app would be used almost daily

Scope: How well-formed is the scope for this app?
How technically challenging will it be to complete this app by the end of the program? Is a stripped-down version of this app still interesting to build? How clearly defined is the product you want to build?

- Both apps would be challenging to build but a stripped-down version will still be interesting to build
